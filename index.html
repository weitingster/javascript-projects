Weiting's Product Building Journal

I'm a Product Leader by trade and part of my job involves working with developers. Sure, I might give some directions on what, and why, we are building. But, watching other people build products is like no other.

A CEO I worked for in the past said to me: "Weiting, one can be a good product manager without knowing how to code. To be a great product manager, learn a little bit. That way, you'll be able to empathize just a tad bit more. For example: when you build your own products, you'll really understand why you need to refactor code."

I pondered on that when I got home.

To be honest, I've tried to learn how to code many, many, many times over the last 5 years. The cycle goes like this: Something happens (e.g. read an article, have a conversation with an engineer, read a job posting, a 1:1 with a CEO) where I end up having a mental breakdown. That sends me into a 'research' mode and I start thinking I need to learn to code again.

I start researching courses. Buy books. Ready to (almost) pay for Bootcamps. Sign up for another newsletter. I plan another "Coding Agenda". I code for a week or two; and then suddenly, another priority pops up. At which point, I deprioritize learning to code. I forget about my enthusiasm to learn to code. All that research goes to waste but I tell myself: "Oh that's ok, the new thing I'm working on is so important!"
...
...
...
And then I come across another one of those "moments". Replay this viscious cycle, again, again, and again. Yes, I failed over, and over, and over again.

Until one day, the company I worked for had a restructuring. I was one of the individuals who were let go. Unfortunate, though it opened my eyes to HUGE opportunities. I knew that if I spent time feeling sorry for myself, I am not progressing. From there, I made a decision that my full-time, 9 to 5, job is now 'building products' for some 30-60 days. OK, you got me, minus some weekends. I even gave myself an extra perk: spice it up with some travelling around the world!

What I noticed: Over the last 5 years, I've been constantly procrastinating on this when I knew I wanted it to learn this skill - so badly. It's not that I haven't learned anything over the last 5 years. It's that I didn't make anything CONCRETE. Nothing that I can say I'm super proud of. I only knew bits and pieces.

Now, there are no excuses. I've made a commitment to myself to build some products before I decide whether I want to work for someone else again.

Current Objectives

  1. To share with others how a non-technical Product Manager, got technical.
  2. To encourage other newbie coders out there that you can learn on your own too. Heck yes.
  3. To commit to 30 days of code, with 5-8 hours a day, while travelling the world.
  4. To show myself that I don't need to pay a dime for a bootcamp because what I really need to do is put theory into action.
  5. To keep track of my coding endeavours so that one day, when I build my own product(s), I can refer back to my learning journey.
